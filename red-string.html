I HAVE THE POWER OF ANIME ON MY SIDE

I made this personal project as a way to experiment with a new style and break out of a rut I was in. I tend to be very colorful and illustrative in my designs, so I wanted to challenge myself to not use any personal illustrations, and keep the color palette restrained. I also wanted to make something fun, that focused on my real life interests. With these two things in mind, I made an exploratory design based around a fake product that helps you find the next anime you should watch based on your interests and current mood.

RED STRING

Next, I moved on to branding. I chose the name Red String for this project. In East Asian folklore, a red string of fate connects destined lovers. This is common theme in many asian stories and embraces the idea that fate will always lead us to our destiny. Although itâ€™s really cheesy, I liked the metaphor of this product becoming the hand of fate that helps us find our next beloved series or movie. The logo for Red String is a woven flower or lucky knot that represents longevity and health, as I hope that this sorting service would bring users emotional wellbeing and happiness. Working within my challenge, I could only use two colors so I chose a pale gray for a main color, and a deep red for accents. I also only allowed myself a single font, so I chose something modern and clean with some visual interest for Hero copy, but high readability for body.
